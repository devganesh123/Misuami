Accuracy, Speed & Rigidity

Accuracy refers to how closely the machine can follow the programmed instructions and produce parts that match the desired dimensions. This is typically measured in micrometers or thousandths of an inch.

Speed refers to how quickly the machine can move and perform operations. This is typically measured in units of distance per minute, such as millimeters per minute or inches per minute.

Rigidity refers to the stiffness and stability of the machine. A more rigid machine will be less prone to vibrations and deformation, which can affect accuracy.
